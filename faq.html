FAQ

I collected here the most common questions from recruiters:

Tell me about yourself and your background

I studied design and HR. I am involved with the web design since 1997 and working professionally since 2004. I designed my first web in 2001 (for a rock band) and my first mobile app in 2009 (for JWT). Working for Globant I had the chance to learn while working for the greatest companies on earth (like Google, Expedia, Lastminute, Sabre, PWC, JWT and many more) Since then I never stopped learning because this world is constantly changing. Worked for startups, big corportations in B2C and B2B products. Started my career as web designer then became visual designer, consultant UX designer, product designer and head of product.

What are you currently working on?

I am currently working for HeadBox. HeadBox is the fastest growing Event Tech brand in the UK, on a mission to reinvent the global events industry through technology. We build human-kind event tech that turns our clients into superheroes. So whether you are a booker, a business or a venue you can unlock brilliant events with HeadBox.

Why do you want a change?

Many reasons: HeadBox is an agency with a very small product team. When I started I was told that they were an tech company with a big product team. The Head of Product who hired me was fired a month after I joined, also one of the PMs. They make too many decisions (mostly all) based on "we promised this to investors", so were little room for the product team to influence. I find the world the events industry a bit shallow. We are not tackling this industry biggest problems.

What are you looking for your next role?

A company that's mature enough to understand and value the product design role and the customer centric approach to problem solving. I'd like to continue developing as a designer and manager, to mentor others and bring a good UX culture into companies. Own a product end to end (or parts of it) and being responsible for my decisions. To work in multidisciplinary diverse teams. A company flexible to understand I have one year old son that sometimes requires my full attention (I currently leave him 8 am in the morning at the nursery and have to pick him up 5:45 pm the latest, at least 3 times a week).

Are you interested in any industry in particular?

DEPENDS. An example: I like fintech companies, but I don't like lending companies. I like B2C apps, but I don't like Revolut. I like healthcare, but some might need a previous experience in that industry. Depends.

What areas of design you think you excel in?

Research, rapid prototyping, testing and visual design. I enjoy illustrating and motion design. I also code a little bit and use daily most new design software. Scaling-up design, mentoring, working closely with developers.

What you think you not so good at?

I am a Product Designer/UX designer, not a UX researcher. I can do many types of research, but if you are looking for deep ethnographic studies you should try hiring anthropologist. UX stole many techniques from anthropology, but anthropologist are better a it.

What you don't like?

When there's no collaboration between teams, when designer work in a silo. Companies that don't value the importance of personal/family time. Companies that don't know what problems are they solving or companies that don't talk to their customers. I don't like to work for agencies or consultancy firms, I did my bit in the past.

Some of the tools I use in my work

After effects, Illustrator, Sketch, Figma, html+css+js, Zeplin, Invision, Principle, Balsamiq, Whimsical, Notion, whiteboard, pen, paper & post-its.

What makes you tick?

To play with my son and watch him grow, to read a great book, to travel, learn a new skill. Music is a big inspiration for everything I do. To solve problems that are causing pain to people. To make people life easier is my ultimate goal.

Are you looking for permanent roles or as contractor?

In the short term it could be contractor but in a big company, long term, permanent.

What's your notice period?

1 Month.

You are argentinian, do you need a Visa?

I have been granted Indefinite leave to remain in the UK. I just need my future employer email to send proof https://right-to-work.service.gov.uk/eua/share-email

Where are you based?

In front of Elephant and Castle station

Salary expectations?

Perm 65-90k depending on resposibilities. Contractor 400-600 p/d. I am flexible, depending on the project and company.

Feedback

If there's a missing question or you have any feedback about my profile don't hesitate to contact me to my phone, linked in or email. Thanks!
