Let me tell you a story...

2018-2019, London.

What's HeadBox?

HeadBox is an events agency with a marketplace that connects people organising events with venues. The proposition is quite simple, guests send briefs to venues and venues respond them. Our agents provide a concierge service to our corporate accounts.

HeadBox day two.

A product manager assigns a task to me:


PM: I need you to fix the filters
ME: What's wrong with them?
PM: We are not sure
ME: Who is supposed to use this?
PM: Guests?
PM: But... who are this people?
PM: Not sure...
ME: How is this helping or not helping people?
PM: ...
ME: Do we have any data about usage?
PM: Not really?
ME: How will this affect in the picture of problems we have? Is this really our biggest priority? Why?
PM: Not sure :(
ME: Have you seen anyone interacting with this?
PM: No :(
ME: Is there any diagram I can see to understand how this is connected to the other products?
PM: No :(
My confidence to make any good decision was close to zero.

HeadBox before the new product team joined

  • Data is all over the place and hard to make sense of it
  • The product was designed in isolation of customer insights.
  • A Head of Product and a freelance designer designed
    • the whole product in a silo to then give a long list of specifications to a remote developement agency to deliver code.
  • This resulted in a marketplace with a 1% conversion rate, a funnel leaking 90% of their users before their first call to action, and a failed CRM that no one wants to use. Head of Product, designer and one PM were fired :(
  • A business model based on commission (how this industry works historically) after the event happened causes more problems and does not help any of the parties involved

New team composition

3 autonomous teams with one designer, one PM and 3 developers.

The first month

  • Organise workshops to gather internal internal and external knowledge about the industry.
  • Gather insights around customer's and non customer's roles, needs, problems and current processes to identify opportunities
  • Create personas based on insights
  • Organise 3 rounds of Interviews with 45 customers
  • Observe users going through the current flow
  • Organise current data sources and generate new ones to understand customer behaviour and problems in the current experience.
  • Educate about a customer centric approach to the entire company by inviting them to interviews and design studios
  • Map current user flows and architecture
    • Current HeadBox guest and host flows
  • Understand and automate manual processes like invoicing and payments
    • Current HeadBox payments flows
  • Fix bugs and mantain the current experience, while launching the platform internationally and launching
  • Launch 2 new products that were already ready to be released
  • Command the customer support chat to gather feedback directly from the customers
  • Analyse the content and relationships of thousands of messages between customers and briefs sent to HeadBox
  • Analyse competitors UX to benchmark our offering
  • Analyse the most common problems sent to support emails
  • Decide on a strategy on how to create UI with reusable components

Communicating insights

HeadBox team composition is mostly 90% sales with an average age of 23 years old. In the beginning we struggled to get people interested in our insights..

Things that worked:

A weekly email with bad news based in data insights but with a joyful tone


Inviting everyone to customer interviews and workshops

Research synthesis in funny videos


All this helped creating a shared understading about the big problems we had to tackle and about how products can we built using insights from data and customer interviews

The big problems identified

After analysing opportunities and prioritising, we decided to tackle the following problems

  • Venues not being able to manage their own listings
  • Guests not being able to find a venue and sending briefs to a support email
  • Guests don't really know what they want and they are overwhelwed by the amount of options
  • Multiple opportunities in the journey to cut out the marketplace
  • The effort put into converting small meeting rooms causes losses
  • Venues wanting more events from us but at the same time not wanting to be spammed with irrelevant events
  • A 1% conversion rate and and drop of 90% in the funnel before the main call to action

How did we tackle this?
